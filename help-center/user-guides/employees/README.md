# Payhawk for Employees

As a Payhawk employee, you use the Payhawk mobile app or web dashboard to pay for business expenses, submit receipts, and raise purchase requests.

## Getting started

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
## Accept your invitation

You'll receive an email from Payhawk with an invitation link. Click it to create your account and set a password.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
## Download the mobile app

Payhawk is available on [iOS](https://apps.apple.com/app/payhawk) and [Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.payhawk). The mobile app is the fastest way to capture receipts the moment you spend.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
## Get your card

Your administrator will issue you a virtual card immediately. A physical card will arrive within 5–7 business days. You can add your virtual card to Apple Pay or Google Pay right away.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## Using your card

Your Payhawk card works anywhere Visa is accepted. To stay within policy:

- Check your **available balance** before a large purchase
- Attach a receipt as soon as you spend — the mobile app will prompt you

{% hint style="warning" %}
Card transactions that exceed your spend policy limit are declined at the point of sale. Contact your administrator to request a temporary limit increase.
{% endhint %}

## Submitting expenses

### From a card transaction

Every card transaction appears in your **Expenses** list automatically. Open it, attach a photo of your receipt, and fill in any required fields (category, notes).

### Out-of-pocket expense

For cash or personal card purchases you need reimbursed:

1. Tap **+ New expense** in the app
2. Enter the amount, date, and merchant
3. Attach a receipt photo
4. Submit for approval

## Raising a purchase request

Before spending on something that requires pre-approval:

1. Go to **Requests → New request**
2. Fill in the vendor, amount, and business justification
3. Submit — your approver will be notified automatically

Once approved, a virtual card is issued for the approved amount.
