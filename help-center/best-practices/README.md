# Best Practices

## For administrators

### Set policies before issuing cards

Configure spend policies and approval workflows before issuing cards. Cards enforce policies at the point of spend — retroactive enforcement is much harder.

### Use teams to organise spend data

Create teams that mirror your cost centres or departments. This makes spend reporting and accounting code mapping significantly cleaner.

### Schedule a monthly reconciliation review

Even with real-time sync, a monthly sweep catches: miscoded expenses, missing receipts (still in draft), and any transactions awaiting approval that have stalled.

## For accountants

### Use bulk actions for routine expenses

Low-value card transactions from known merchants (e.g. a recurring SaaS subscription) rarely need line-by-line review. Use **Bulk approve** after a visual scan, and save your detailed review time for high-value or flagged items.

### Map your full chart of accounts before go-live

Incomplete accounting code mapping is the most common source of export errors. Complete the mapping in **Settings → Integrations** before your first export, not after.

### Configure AI Office suggestions before month-end pressure

Train AI Office coding suggestions by reviewing and confirming the first 20–30 expenses manually. The model improves quickly with feedback.

## For employees

### Photograph receipts immediately

Receipt quality degrades fast — snap a photo the moment you spend, before the paper fades or goes missing. The Payhawk mobile app's camera opens directly from the transaction.

### Submit expenses the same week

Expenses submitted more than 30 days after the transaction date are flagged for review. Same-week submission keeps your review queue clean and reimbursements faster.

### Use the travel agent for bookings

Booking travel through Payhawk's Travel AI Agent means your itinerary, cost, and travel category are all captured automatically — no manual expense submission needed.
